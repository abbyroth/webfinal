How Did We Get Here?

Heres the situation.

The number one pollutant in our oceans is plastic. Think about how many plastic things you use on a daily that are just single use. From plastic straws to coffee cups, we use a lot of plastic. Now imagine the amount of plastic you use daily and multiply it just by the population of your country. That is a lot of plastic!

According to UNEP, "Of the seven billion tonnes of plastic waste generated globally so far, less than 10 per cent has been recycled." Not only are we producing plastic at an alarming rate, we are doing very little to manage the plastic that is already present on the Earth. Without massive change in how much we produce and what we do with what is already produced, we are making it exponentially harder to manange the situation.

What ways can you help? One person may not seem like a lot, but every person doing their part contributes a ton. A good start is by limiting the use of single use plastics such as plastic water bottles and straws. These plastics add up and are thrown away with little thought. Give these items more thought as you think about their overall impact and even search for ways to reuse or repurpose these items
